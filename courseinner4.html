COURSE OVERVIEW

Cyber forensic, also known as digital forensics, is a specialized field that involves the application of investigative techniques to gather and analyze digital evidence in the aftermath of cyber incidents. Its primary goal is to uncover, preserve, and interpret electronic data in a forensically sound manner, ensuring that the evidence is admissible in legal proceedings. Cyber forensic professionals use a variety of tools and methodologies to investigate a wide range of cybercrimes, including data breaches, hacking incidents, and digital fraud.

In the realm of cyber forensic, one critical aspect is the preservation of evidence integrity. Forensic experts employ meticulous procedures to collect and safeguard digital evidence, maintaining an unaltered chain of custody to guarantee its authenticity in court. This process includes the identification of digital footprints, analysis of system logs, and recovery of deleted or encrypted data. The information gathered during cyber forensic investigations can provide insights into the methods used by attackers, the extent of the compromise, and potential vulnerabilities in the digital infrastructure.

Cyber forensic plays a crucial role in both reactive and proactive cybersecurity strategies. Beyond post-incident analysis, it contributes to the development of preventive measures by identifying patterns of cyber threats and vulnerabilities. With the increasing frequency and sophistication of cybercrimes, the demand for skilled cyber forensic professionals continues to grow. Their expertise is essential not only for law enforcement agencies but also for private organizations seeking to protect sensitive data and ensure the integrity of their digital assets in an interconnected and technology-driven world.
